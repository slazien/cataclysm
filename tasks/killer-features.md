### Pain Point 1: "I have no idea how to interpret my data"
The #1 complaint. Drivers buy logging equipment, collect data, then stare at squiggly lines. "Had no effing idea how to use it." Need "race engineer level knowledge" to extract value.
> **Cataclysm answer:** AI coaching that translates data into plain English. "Brake 15m earlier into Turn 5" beats "here's a speed vs distance graph."

### Pain Point 2: Multi-device setup complexity
Phone + GPS + OBD dongle + power cords + mount + camera = "a pain." RAM mounts flying off during hot laps (safety hazard). Bluetooth disconnects.
> **Cataclysm answer:** Software-only solution working with a single GPS device.

### Pain Point 3: Video/data sync is broken
"The latency or lag of data rendered in video is so much that information is useless." GoPro integration constantly breaking.
> **Cataclysm answer:** Focus on data analysis first. Animated track replays replace video overlay.

### Pain Point 4: Apps break with OS updates
Harry's Lap Timer "reliability issues." Porsche Track Precision App "extremely unreliable."
> **Cataclysm answer:** Web-based platform immune to mobile OS breakage.

### Pain Point 5: Subscription fatigue
Garmin Vault $9.99/month, Apex Pro $678/year. Users hate recurring fees on top of hardware costs.
> **Cataclysm answer:** Generous free tier with pay-per-use AI coaching.

### Pain Point 6: No unified ecosystem
AIM, RaceChrono, TrackAddict data don't talk to each other. Sharing with coach requires exporting files and emailing.
> **Cataclysm answer:** Cloud platform importing from multiple sources with easy sharing.

### Pain Point 7: Gadgets before fundamentals
Multiple experienced drivers advise "get instruction FIRST." Beginners get overwhelmed with raw data.
> **Cataclysm answer:** AI coaching acts as instructor first (fundamentals), before raw data. Progressive disclosure.
